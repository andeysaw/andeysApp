In the morning, we checked out the Current River and then drove north for a while to reach Johnson's Shut-ins State Park. The shut-ins turned out to be a scenic part of the Black River filled with big rocks in which hundreds of people were playing. We hiked a short loop trail for views of the shut-ins, then left for Taum Sauk Mountain Park.

I wanted to hike a loop trail to see Mina Sauk Falls, but people who had just taken the trail told me it was dry. Instead, my brother and I went along a short paved route until we reached, to my surprise, the spot that was marked as the highest point in Missouri, at only a little over 1700 feet in elevation. Having come from California, I wasn't impressed.

Knowing we were on the highest mountain around, we climbed up a rickety old observation tower to see the expansive view of the green Ozark Mountains.

On our way home, we made one more stop in Chester, Illinois, to eat our last canned soup by an historic covered bridge.
