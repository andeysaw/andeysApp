I had a breakfast of oatmeal before taking the Metro bus and rail to Smithsonian Station. Due to the rain and low clouds, I decided to delay going to the Washington Monument and instead lined up to get a timed ticket for the Bureau of Engraving and Printing from its small ticket booth. I talked to a guy from Ohio in line while waiting and then got a ticket for the 9 AM tour soon after the booth opened.

Having time until the tour and not wanting to stay outside in the rain, I went inside the Holocaust Museum Cafe as soon as it opened and just bought an orange to leisurely consume. I got to the entrance to the Bureau of Engraving and Printing in time for the tour that started with a video of the money making process. We then went through each room of the process, seeing it from an enclosed walkway while a guide narrated. I saw large machines and pallets of paper destined to become money.

The tour ended at a gift shop with unique gifts like money with certain serial numbers and reproductions of historic government printings. I bought a postcard and then headed back to the Metrorail to take the train to Dupont Circle for a free admission day of the permanent collection of the Phillips Collection. Generally, I am not a fan of modern art, but I felt the collection had some good pieces. I started in the more modern building and then got led into the old house complete with appropriate furniture for each room.

The collection showed paintings by many famous artists such as Cezanne, Van Gogh, Renoir, Picasso and Matisse as well a small dim room dedicated to Rothko pieces. I made sure to go to every room except the special exhibit, which cost extra. Then, I hopped back onto the Metrorail to eat at Ben's Chili Bowl. Inside, I found the place crowded and lively and sat down at the counter. A note boasted that Bill Cosby and Barrack Obama could eat there free, even though Obama has paid for his meal.

After I figured out how to correctly get my order in the hectic place, I enjoyed chili half-smokes and cheese fries. When done, I left and walked to the African-American Civil War Memorial to take a quick look at it before getting on the Metrorail again. From the Archives Station, I ambled through the Sculpture Garden of the National Gallery of Art and then made my way to the Smithsonian Museum of Natural History where I checked out the new Ocean Hall.

Underneath the enormous hanging North Atlantic Right Whale, I saw interesting specimens big and small such as a giant octopus and creatures of the deep ocean. I then continued walking around, briefly noting other museum displays, in particular the Hall of Mammals with its life size models. I also spent time in an exhibit showing human bones while explaining how to read them for clues to their history. However, I skipped the enclosed butterfly garden because it charged admission.

In light rain, I next made my way to the newly remolded Museum of American History where I encountered a line. I took a photo of an Asian girl with the Washington Monument at her request while there. Inside, I admired the new three level Flag Hall that focused on a metallic abstract rendition of the American flag. A small opening below the flag led through a dim corridor to a display of the Star Spangled Banner, laid out at a low angle.

Going upstairs, I took a look at at an interesting exhibit titled “America at War” that showed artifacts and props while giving information about many of America's conflicts. Also nearby, I found a display of heavily damaged steel from the World Trade Center of New York. Then, back in Flag Hall, I briefly watched students and others learn to fold a huge flag.

I noticed an impressive huge dollhouse done by Faith Bradford and then spotted Stephen Colbert's portrait. I had been on the lookout for that. After passing other interest small displays such as Kermit the Frog, I briefly spent time at the “Within These Walls…” exhibit that told a story of American families using a partially reconstructed house and then spent considerable time going through the large “America on the Move” exhibit about transportation throughout American history.

Next, I found Julia Child's entire kitchen impressively displayed and then wandered around the remaining displays, including a C-3PO movie costume used in Return of the Jedi. I finished my visit with a look at the collection of First Ladies fashions before taking the Metrorail to the National Portrait Gallery to finish seeing that collection.

After finishing up looking at the rest of the portraits, I went to the Smithsonian American Art Museum within the same building. It had already felt like a long day, so I briefly checked out each of the many rooms and then focused on whatever caught my eye. I made sure to include George Catlin's Indian Gallery as one of the notable pieces. I finished at the contemporary art section where Nam June Paik's video installations particularly stood out.

I took the Metrolink back to Silver Spring where I met up with my friend Darlene at the Mandalay Restaurant and Cafe. We hung out and talked and I ordere mixed noodles, chicken and sticky rice. After that lovely dinner, I took the Y9 bus back to Aspen Hill. I got back kind of late but made time to write on postcards before going to sleep.
