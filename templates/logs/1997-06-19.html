In the morning, we went to the medical center at Prince of Songkla University to get my teeth examined. First, though, I ate something, knowing I may noy be able to do so later. The center turned out to be beautiful, surrounded by gardens. Inside it was clean, light, and airy.

I was surprised that the first floor was open to the outside air, but it seemed clean. I had to leave my shoes behind for slippers once I entered. X-rays revealed that my lower left wisdom tooth was putting pressure on the rest of my teeth. They set me down in a chair, applied local anesthetic, then simply pulled out the offending tooth.

After the quick pull, I was given pain medication and something to absorb the blood. I felt fine, for the most part, and was really happy to see the bill. It cost me only about 200 Baht, which was about eight U.S. dollars.

My friends had waited for me and met me on my way out, asking if I was okay. I was fine, so off we went to explore the town a little bit. Hat Yai didn't turn out to be all that interesting compared to other Thai towns I had visited in past trips. However, it was new to my friends. Particularly interesting to see were the spirit houses commonly found in front of dwellings.

We made our way to the railway station, taking a songthaew, which was a modified pickup truck with two facing seats on the truck bed. We bought tickets for a sleeper train to Chumpon, then boarded for our trip north.
